4. Your Rights Under GDPR

Under the General Data Protection Regulation, you have the following rights:

  • Right of Access (Art. 15): Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you. Use the "Export Data" feature in your profile.
  • Right to Rectification (Art. 16): Correct inaccurate or incomplete data via your profile settings.
  • Right to Erasure (Art. 17): Request complete deletion of your account and all associated data. Use the "Delete Account" feature or contact us.
  • Right to Data Portability (Art. 20): Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable JSON format.
  • Right to Object (Art. 21): Object to processing based on legitimate interests, including direct marketing.
  • Right to Restrict Processing (Art. 18): Request limitation of processing in specific circumstances.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time via cookie preferences or by contacting us.

To exercise any right, email privacy@ledoraai.com. We respond within 30 days.

5. HIPAA Compliance

HIPAA

Ledora AI processes Protected Health Information (PHI) in compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

5.1 PHI Protection

  • All health data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256).
  • Access controls enforce minimum-necessary principles.
  • Audit logs track all PHI access and modifications.

5.2 Business Associate Agreements

We maintain Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all third-party service providers that process PHI, including Google Cloud/Firebase and AI model providers.

5.3 Breach Notification

In the event of a breach involving unsecured PHI, we will notify affected individuals within 60 days and report to the HHS Secretary as required by the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule.

6. NIS2 Compliance

NIS2

As a provider of digital health services within the EU, Ledora AI adheres to the NIS2 Directive (Directive (EU) 2022/2555).

6.1 Security Measures

  • Risk-based approach to information security management.
  • Regular security assessments and penetration testing.
  • Incident response procedures with defined escalation paths.
  • Supply chain security evaluation for all third-party services.
  • Multi-factor authentication and role-based access controls.

6.2 Incident Reporting

Significant security incidents are reported to the relevant national CSIRT within 24 hours of detection, with a full incident report provided within 72 hours, in compliance with NIS2 Article 23.
